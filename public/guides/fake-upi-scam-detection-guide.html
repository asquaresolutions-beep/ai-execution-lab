Fake UPI Scam Detection Guide

How to tell a real UPI payment from a forged "payment successful" screenshot — in 30 seconds. From ScamCheck by A Square Solutions.

Scammers send a fake "payment successful" screenshot to pressure you into shipping goods, releasing a service, or refunding money before any money actually reaches you. A screenshot can be edited in seconds — it is never proof of payment.

The one rule that beats every fake screenshot

Only the credit in YOUR own bank or UPI app is proof of payment. Not an SMS, not a screenshot, not a "reference number" the other person reads out. If it isn't in your account, you weren't paid.

The 30-second check

1. Don't trust the image. Treat every "paid" / "success" / "pending" screenshot as unverified.
2. Open your own bank or UPI app. Check your real balance and transaction history yourself — don't rely on what they show or tell you.
3. Match the UTR + timestamp. A real payment has a UTR / reference number and an exact time. Confirm both appear in your statement, not just theirs.
4. Never release goods or refunds on a screenshot. Wait until the money is settled in your account. "It's processing" is the scammer's favourite line.
5. When unsure, run it through ScamCheck. Paste the message or upload the screenshot at scamcheck.asquaresolution.com for an instant risk read.

Red flags in a fake UPI screenshot

  • Urgency — "I've paid, please ship now / refund now."
  • The amount is oddly specific or unusually high for a stranger.
  • Bank/UPI logos or fonts look slightly off, misaligned, or low-resolution.
  • A "NPCI" or "bank" confirmation that arrives from the buyer, not your own app.
  • They refuse a small test transfer or a quick phone verification.
  • The reference/UTR number doesn't appear anywhere in your account.

If you've already been scammed

  1. Stop all further transfers immediately.
  2. Call your bank's fraud line and ask to freeze/raise a dispute.
  3. In India, report at cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930 as soon as possible — faster reporting improves recovery odds.
  4. Keep all screenshots, numbers, and chat logs as evidence.

This is general safety information, not legal or financial advice. If money or sensitive details may be involved, contact your bank and national fraud authority immediately.
