An authority flywheel is a system where each loop of activity produces more authority than the last. The value compounds rather than accumulating linearly.

The A Square Solutions flywheel has seven active loops. Each loop is a mechanism: it takes an input, produces an output, and that output feeds another loop. When all seven loops are active simultaneously, the system self-reinforces.

This document maps each loop, its current operational state, and what activating it at full capacity unlocks.

---

## The Full Flywheel Map

```
PRODUCTION WORK (asquaresolution.com)
         ↓ generates
EXECUTION RECORDS (Lab — logs, failures, case studies)
         ↓ extracted into
LINKEDIN DISTRIBUTION → Audience reach → Backlinks → External authority
         ↓ published at
AI EXECUTION LAB (GEO-optimized corpus)
         ↓ indexed by
AI RETRIEVAL SYSTEMS → Citations → Inbound traffic → New practitioners
         ↓ observe
OPERATIONAL FAILURES → Filed in failure archive → Pattern intelligence
         ↓ inform
CASE STUDIES + PLAYBOOKS → Evidence of outcomes → Trust signals
         ↓ drive
PRODUCT AUTHORITY (TrustSeal, ScamCheck) → Commercial outcomes
         ↓ generates
MORE PRODUCTION WORK → More operational records → Loop restarts
```

The flywheel has no beginning and no end. Each loop strengthens every other. The constraint at any point is: which loop is currently weakest?

## Loop 1: WordPress → Lab

**Mechanism:** `asquaresolution.com` is an active commercial WordPress site with organic search traffic. Every page now carries three cross-property signals: the "AI Lab" nav link, the "OUR WORK" footer widget, and Schema.org `owns` array that declares the Lab as an owned property.

**What this does:**
- Transfers domain authority from the WordPress site (older, more backlinks) to the Lab
- Creates a topical link from an active production site to the Lab's specific content areas
- Tells search engines that the entity operating `asquaresolution.com` also operates `lab.asquaresolution.com`

**Current state (as of 2026-05-20):**
- Nav link: ✓ present sitewide
- Footer widget: ✓ 3 ecosystem links sitewide
- Schema `owns`: ✓ Organization + WebSite deployed
- Internal links: ✓ 5 top posts linked with topical anchors
- About page: ✓ "Built in Public" paragraph + Lab link

**What full activation looks like:**
- 20+ top posts with contextual Lab links (current: 5)
- About page expanded with Lab mention in hero (current: bottom of page)
- Homepage Lab section above fold (current: below fold)

## Loop 2: Lab → LinkedIn

**Mechanism:** Lab content (logs, failures, case studies) is extracted into LinkedIn posts following the five post templates in the [LinkedIn Content Engine](/docs/linkedin-content-engine). Each post links back to the specific Lab record.

**What this does:**
- LinkedIn content drives practitioner awareness of the Lab
- Link-backs create attribution signals for search engines
- Post engagement provides social proof signals
- Comments from practitioners add topical entity signals to the LinkedIn post and by extension to the Lab

**Current state:**
- Templates: ✓ defined (5 types)
- Extraction workflow: ✓ documented
- Content inventory: 12 failures + 12 logs + 8 case studies = 32+ posts available
- Actual posting cadence: Not yet active

**What full activation looks like:**
- 3 posts per week consistently
- UTM tracking from LinkedIn → Lab in GA4
- Comments replied to with links to related Lab content
- 100+ Lab backlinks from LinkedIn posts over 12 months

## Loop 3: Failures → GEO

**Mechanism:** The failure archive contains 12 resolved production failures with root cause classifications, resolution times, prevention patterns, and severity scores. Each failure answers specific "why does X fail" and "how to fix X in Y context" queries that practitioners search for.

**What this does:**
- Failure reports answer operational questions that have no other authoritative source
- Answer-first structure + entity density makes failures high-retrieval targets for AI search
- Failure patterns create a navigational corpus: a practitioner searching for "LiteSpeed cache debugging" finds a specific, dated, resolved incident

**Current state:**
- 12 failures scored: ✓
- Root cause classifications: ✓
- Prevention patterns: ✓
- GEO structure (answer-first): partial — some failures need structural improvement
- Entity density scoring: not yet audited

**What full activation looks like:**
- 20+ failures (enough to cover all major failure categories in the stack)
- Every failure with at least Level 2 evidence
- Answer-first intro on every failure report
- All failures cross-linked to related playbooks and docs

## Loop 4: Logs → Case Studies

**Mechanism:** Execution logs document what happened step by step. Case studies extract the architectural insight and measurable outcome from the logs. Case studies carry the strategic intelligence that logs contain but don't make explicit.

**What this does:**
- Case studies are more searchable and retrievable than logs (more narrative, more keywords, better structure)
- They convert log authority into strategic insight authority
- A practitioner arriving at a case study via search has a higher intent than one arriving at a log — they are usually deciding whether to do the same thing

**Current state:**
- 8 case studies: ✓
- 12 logs: ✓ (4 logs not yet converted to case studies)
- Log → case study conversion rate: ~60%

**What full activation looks like:**
- Every major deployment log has an associated case study
- Case studies reference logs as evidence sources
- Case studies are the primary GEO surface for strategic queries

---

## Loop 5: Case Studies → Products

**Mechanism:** Case studies that document the development and operation of TrustSeal and ScamCheck convert product authority into commercial trust signals. A practitioner who reads the ScamCheck architecture build and understands the engineering decisions is more likely to trust the product.

**What this does:**
- Reduces the sales burden — the case study does the explaining
- Creates topical authority for the product's domain (scam detection, trust verification)
- Links the Lab as the operational record of product development

**Current state:**
- ScamCheck architecture build case study: ✓
- TrustSeal architecture build case study: ✓
- Cross-links from products to Lab: not yet implemented (products are separate Vite deployments)

**What full activation looks like:**
- Lab link in the footer of TrustSeal and ScamCheck
- "Built on documented operational practice" statement on product landing pages
- Case study links on product pages pointing to the Lab

---

## Loop 6: Products → Authority

**Mechanism:** TrustSeal and ScamCheck are production AI tools serving real users. The operational authority of the Lab compounds with the commercial credibility of live, used products. A Lab that documents the operation of real tools is more authoritative than one that documents hypothetical systems.

**What this does:**
- External users of TrustSeal and ScamCheck are potential Lab readers
- Product traffic diversifies the Lab's traffic sources beyond pure search
- Commercial deployment creates "production at scale" evidence that pure documentation labs lack

**Current state:**
- TrustSeal: production, serving users ✓
- ScamCheck: production, serving users ✓
- Cross-property navigation: basic (no ecosystem footer on products)

**What full activation looks like:**
- Ecosystem footer on TrustSeal and ScamCheck
- "Operational record" link from product pages to the Lab
- Product incident reports in the Lab failure archive (each production incident = a failure report)

---

## Loop 7: Authority → Search Visibility → Operational Evidence

**Mechanism:** As the Lab's authority increases through the above loops, it gains search visibility for operational queries. This brings more practitioners to the Lab who then observe the operational evidence standard — screenshots, gate results, evidence-first structure — and treat the Lab as a citation target, linking to it from their own content.

**What this does:**
- External links increase domain authority
- More organic traffic creates more social proof signals
- The operational evidence standard differentiates the Lab from documentation sites that summarize others' research

**Current state:**
- Authority: early stage
- Search visibility: growing
- External citations: nascent
- GEO retrieval: partial coverage (see `/ops/seo` for current query coverage)

**What full activation looks like:**
- Lab cited by AI systems answering "how to X with Claude Code" queries
- External practitioners linking to specific failure reports and playbooks
- GEO coverage 80%+ of targeted query categories

## The Compounding Timeline

**Months 1-3 (infrastructure):** All flywheel loops at partial activation. Authority building slowly. Each new operational record adds to the corpus.

**Months 3-6 (traction):** LinkedIn cadence creates consistent inbound. AI retrieval systems begin citing failure reports for operational queries. First external links appear.

**Months 6-12 (compounding):** GEO coverage reaches 60%+. Lab cited across AI search results for Claude Code, LiteSpeed, WordPress REST API, schema.org queries. External practitioners link to specific failure reports.

**Year 2+ (authority):** The Lab is a canonical reference for its topic areas. Each new failure or log adds to a corpus that AI systems rely on for operational answers. New content gets indexed and cited within days. The flywheel spins without manual acceleration.
