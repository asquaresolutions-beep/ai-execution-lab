This is the capstone: ScamCheck now grows itself — detecting trending scams, generating quality-gated pages, distributing alerts, attracting links, and retaining users — inside a hard cost cap.

## What shipped (mapped to the brief)
- **A) Autonomous publishing** — autopilot (trending + freshness + dedup) now adds a **pre-publish quality gate** and a **Vertex daily-budget circuit breaker**; low-quality clusters are left unstamped to retry later.
- **B) Hindi expansion** — bilingual pages/OG already; added bilingual trending strip + Hindi entity names in schema/graph.
- **C) AI Overview** — scam-vs-safe **comparison block**, **entity extraction** (schema `mentions`), and **machine-readable trust** (`trustScore`, `publishingPrinciples`, `creditText`).
- **D) Retention** — email-alert subscribe API (rate-limited), localStorage **watchlist**, and browser-notification opt-in.
- **E) Trust layer** — editorial policy, AI disclosure, methodology, transparency, about — indexed, schema'd, linked from Article `publishingPrinciples`.
- **F) Conversion** — high-intent topics surfaced via `convertingTopics`; ad-free for paid users (prior phase).
- **G) Distribution automation** — X **threads** + **carousel** hooks; autopilot stores a per-bundle `distribution_alerts` payload.
- **H) Data moat** — canonical **entity registry + relationship graph** (`entities.ts`), aliases, cluster fingerprints.
- **I) Cost protection** — budget breaker, cache-first AI, increment counters, throttles, graceful degradation.
- **J) Analytics** — Discover candidates, fastest-growing entities, topic leverage, **backlink topics**, **converting topics** via `/api/scam-intel/growth`.

## Cost posture
Auto-publishing is bounded by `BUNDLES_PER_DAY`, `AUTOPILOT_PER_RUN`, and a hard `DAILY_BUDGET_USD` breaker. Pages stay SSG; real-time data is CDN-cached; distribution/entities/analytics are deterministic. Stays within **Vercel Hobby + Firestore Spark + a few dollars/month of Vertex** (cap-enforced).

## Estimates (planning, not guarantees)

**Organic growth potential.** ~250 evergreen pages today, growing autonomously as new scams trend (quality-gated). With maturation (3–6 months) and the freshness/authority loops below, a plausible trajectory is **low-thousands → tens-of-thousands of monthly organic clicks**, weighted toward tier-1 types and Hindi long-tail.

**Authority growth.** The trust layer + citations + entity graph materially lift E-E-A-T. Expect steady gains in rankings for "is this a scam / verdict" intent and rising AI-Overview citation frequency as the machine-readable trust/entity signals are consumed.

**Discover visibility growth.** Freshness (live trending/active badges) + dedicated OG images + clear authorship are exactly Discover's signals. Trending pages should become Discover-eligible within weeks; Discover then contributes **bursty, high-volume spikes** (especially around seasonal hubs).

**Backlink accumulation.** Embeddable widgets convert ScamCheck into distributed infrastructure. Modest adoption (50–300 embedding domains over 6–12 months) → **dozens to a few hundred referring domains** — the dominant off-page driver for a young authority site.

## The biggest compounding loops
1. **Detect → publish → rank → attract reports → detect more.** Trending detection feeds pages; pages rank and pull in more scam reports; more reports sharpen detection. *(Self-reinforcing data flywheel.)*
2. **Freshness → crawl → Discover → traffic → freshness.** Real-time trending keeps pages re-crawled; Discover rewards freshness with traffic; traffic surfaces new scams. 
3. **Widgets → backlinks → authority → rankings → more embeds.** Each embed is a dofollow link; authority lifts rankings; higher visibility earns more embeds.
4. **Authority/trust → AI-Overview citations → brand → direct + branded search.** Machine-readable trust + citations make ScamCheck the cited source; citations build brand; brand drives durable direct/branded demand.

These loops **intersect** (reports feed detection feeds pages feeds links feeds authority feeds citations feeds traffic feeds reports). A competitor copying any single loop doesn't get the compounding — that intersection is the moat.
