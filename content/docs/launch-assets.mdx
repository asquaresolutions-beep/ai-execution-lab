## Social Positioning

### What this platform is

- A public execution record, not a marketing site
- Evidence of work quality, not claims about work quality
- An ongoing operational log, not a finished product
- A GEO-native authority platform — structured for AI search citation

### What this platform is not

- A tutorial site
- A course platform (the Tracks are execution-focused curricula, not courses)
- An agency portfolio
- A blog

### Key differentiators (use in positioning copy)

1. **Real failures documented** — most platforms hide failures; we document them
2. **Session-level granularity** — execution logs show the actual work, not summaries
3. **Production provenance** — every case study comes from live running systems
4. **Operational rhythm** — this is updated continuously, not periodically polished

### Tone guide

- Technical but not academic
- Direct, not promotional
- Evidence-first, not claim-first
- Honest about failures, not defensive
- No hedging, no false modesty

Wrong: "We're excited to share our journey of building AI tools."
Right: "Three production failures. All resolved. Documented with root cause."

Wrong: "We're leveraging cutting-edge AI to transform workflows."
Right: "We use Claude Code to build Next.js applications. Here's what that looks like."
