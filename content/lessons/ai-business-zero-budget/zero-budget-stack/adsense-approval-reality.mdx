AdSense approval is one of the most searched topics for beginner content businesses. Most advice online is either outdated, vague, or based on successful applications that don't reflect typical outcomes.

This lesson gives you the specific standards Google actually applies, the real reasons applications fail, and how to know when your site is genuinely ready.

---

## What AdSense Is

Google AdSense pays you to display Google's ads on your website. When a visitor sees or clicks an ad, you earn a portion of what the advertiser paid.

**Revenue model:**
- **RPM (Revenue Per Mille):** How much you earn per 1,000 page views
- Typical RPM ranges: ₹30–150 for Indian traffic, $3–15 for US/UK traffic, $8–40 for finance/tech topics

**Example at Indian traffic RPM:**
- 10,000 monthly page views × ₹80 RPM ÷ 1,000 = ₹800/month
- 50,000 monthly page views × ₹80 RPM ÷ 1,000 = ₹4,000/month
- 100,000 monthly page views × ₹80 RPM ÷ 1,000 = ₹8,000/month

AdSense is a volume play. At Indian traffic rates, meaningful income requires significant traffic. This is not a "quick money" model — it's a compounding asset.

## What AdSense Actually Requires

Google's official requirements are:

1. Your site must comply with AdSense Program Policies
2. You must be 18 or older (or have a parent/guardian who is)
3. Your site must have original content

That's deliberately vague. Here's what the review process actually checks:

### Content requirements

**Minimum content threshold:** Google's reviewers need to see enough content to evaluate quality. In practice: a site with fewer than 15–20 substantive posts often gets rejected as "insufficient content." This doesn't mean 15 thin 300-word posts — it means 15 posts that each fully answer a search query.

**Original content:** Content that is clearly AI-generated with no human editing, content copied from other sites, or thin rewrites of existing articles will fail review.

**Content quality signals:** Google is evaluating whether your site provides real value to users. Posts that are specific, accurate, and useful pass. Posts that are generic summaries of generic summaries don't.

### Technical requirements

- **Custom domain:** Free subdomain sites (blogspot.com, wordpress.com free tier) are not eligible
- **Working site:** All pages load, no broken navigation, no "under construction" sections
- **HTTPS:** Your site must be accessible via HTTPS (modern hosting handles this automatically)
- **Contact page or About page:** Demonstrates the site is run by a real entity
- **Privacy policy:** Required, especially if you're collecting any user data

### Traffic requirements

Google officially says AdSense does not require a minimum traffic threshold. In practice: very low traffic (under 500 monthly visitors) often results in rejection because there isn't enough user behavior data for the review.

The practical minimum: 500–1,000 monthly organic visitors from Google Search before applying. This takes 3–6 months for a new site.

## Why Applications Get Rejected

These are the real reasons, in order of frequency:

**1. Insufficient content**
The most common reason. Solution: don't apply until you have 20+ substantive published posts.

**2. Low-value content**
Posts that don't fully answer any question, are too short (under 600 words), or are clearly generated without human editing. Solution: editing pass on every post.

**3. Policy violations**
Adult content, gambling, violent content, copyright infringement, misleading claims. Ensure your content doesn't touch these areas.

**4. Navigation or UX problems**
Menu links that don't work, pages that error out, broken internal links. Solution: test every navigation element before applying.

**5. No About/Contact page**
Google wants to confirm a real entity runs the site. Add an About page (brief is fine) and a Contact page with at least an email address.

**6. Privacy Policy missing**
Required for any site that may serve ads (which collect data). Generate one using a free privacy policy generator and publish it at `yourdomain.com/privacy-policy`.

**7. Missing legal pages**
Terms of service and a disclaimer are not strictly required but reduce rejection risk.

## The Pre-Application Checklist

Before applying, every item on this list should be complete:

**Content:**
- [ ] Minimum 20 published posts
- [ ] Each post is 600+ words and specifically answers a search query
- [ ] Every post has been through a human editing pass
- [ ] No copied or barely-modified content from other sources
- [ ] Content is in a consistent niche (not random unrelated topics)

**Pages:**
- [ ] About page — who runs the site and why
- [ ] Contact page — email address or contact form
- [ ] Privacy Policy page — use a generator if needed
- [ ] Terms of Service page (optional but beneficial)

**Technical:**
- [ ] Site accessible at HTTPS custom domain
- [ ] All navigation links work
- [ ] No pages returning errors (check in Google Search Console → Indexing)
- [ ] Mobile version loads and reads correctly
- [ ] No broken images on published posts

**Traffic:**
- [ ] At least 500 monthly organic visitors (check Google Analytics)
- [ ] Search Console shows pages indexed and receiving impressions

## Applying for AdSense

1. Go to adsense.google.com
2. Sign in with your Google account
3. Enter your site URL
4. Complete the account setup (country, payment information, tax information)
5. Add the AdSense code snippet to your site
   - **WordPress:** Site Kit by Google plugin adds it automatically after you connect AdSense
   - **Next.js:** Add the snippet to your layout file or use the `next/script` component
6. Wait for review

**Review time:** Typically 1–3 weeks. Google emails you the result.

---

## If Your Application Is Rejected

Google usually gives a reason. The most common rejection messages:

**"Insufficient content"** — Publish more. Wait another 2–3 months. Reapply.

**"Site does not comply with AdSense policies"** — Review the specific policy cited. Fix the issue. Don't reapply until it's resolved.

**"Site is under construction"** — All published content must be complete. Remove "coming soon" sections or posts before applying.

You can reapply after fixing the issues. There's no penalty for rejection — but rapid repeated applications look suspicious. Fix the stated issues, wait 4–6 weeks, then reapply.

## What RPM to Realistically Expect

RPM varies significantly by:
- **Traffic geography:** US/UK/Australia traffic → $5–20 RPM. Indian traffic → ₹40–150 RPM.
- **Topic:** Finance, legal, software → high RPM. Entertainment, general lifestyle → low RPM.
- **Ad placement:** Above-the-fold placements earn more than sidebar ads.

**Conservative estimates for Indian traffic:**

| Monthly Page Views | RPM | Monthly Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| 10,000 | ₹60 | ₹600 |
| 30,000 | ₹70 | ₹2,100 |
| 50,000 | ₹80 | ₹4,000 |
| 100,000 | ₹90 | ₹9,000 |

These are conservative estimates. Finance and software niches in India can achieve ₹100–200 RPM. Entertainment/general content runs ₹20–60 RPM.

**The honest math:** At Indian traffic, you need 50,000+ monthly page views to generate meaningful monthly income from AdSense alone. That's a 6–18 month timeline for a new site built with consistent content.

## When AdSense Is the Right Model

AdSense makes sense when:
- Your content is informational (how-to, guides, comparisons)
- You're targeting topics with advertiser demand
- You're building for long-term organic traffic
- You don't want to handle customers, fulfillment, or sales

AdSense is the wrong model when:
- Your audience is small but highly targeted (better: direct sponsorships, affiliate products)
- Your content is in a low-RPM niche
- You need revenue within 3 months (AdSense takes 6–18 months to build to meaningful income)
- You can sell something directly to your audience at higher margins

---

<Callout type="warn" title="AdSense is not passive income at first">
"Passive income from AdSense" is the end state of a content business that required months of active work. The passive part comes after the active work of building traffic. A site with 100,000 monthly visitors running on 200+ posts took 12–24 months and hundreds of hours to build. The income is passive at that point — but the work that created the asset was not.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info" title="AdSense alternative: Ezoic">
Ezoic is an AdSense alternative that often achieves higher RPM through better ad optimization. It has a lower barrier than Ezoic used to have (no traffic minimum currently). It requires technical setup on your site but is free to use (they take a percentage of ad revenue). Once your AdSense account is established and has some traffic history, Ezoic is worth evaluating as a higher-RPM alternative.
</Callout>
